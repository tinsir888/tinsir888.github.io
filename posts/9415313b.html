Lattices

Lattices are integral combinations of linearly independent vectors:

{∑i=1kλibi∣λ1,⋯ ,λk∈Z}\left\{\sum_{i=1}^k\lambda_i\mathbf b_i\vert\lambda_1,\cdots,\lambda_k\in\Z\right\}{i=1∑k​λi​bi​∣λ1​,⋯,λk​∈Z}

where b1,⋯ ,bk∈Rn\mathbf b_1,\cdots,\mathbf b_k\in\R^nb1​,⋯,bk​∈Rn are linearly independent vectors.

Another equivalent definition: a lattice is a discrete subgroup of Rn\R^nRn.

If k=nk=nk=n the lattice has full rank. Without specification, the we consider lattice with full rank.

Let B∈Rn×n\mathbf B\in\R^{n\times n}B∈Rn×n be the matrix that has the basis vector b1,⋯ ,bn\mathbf b_1,\cdots,\mathbf b_nb1​,⋯,bn​ as columns, the lattice is

Λ(B)={∑i=1kλibi∣λ1,⋯ ,λk∈Z}.\Lambda(\mathbf B)=\left\{\sum_{i=1}^k\lambda_i\mathbf b_i\vert\lambda_1,\cdots,\lambda_k\in\Z\right\}.Λ(B)={i=1∑k​λi​bi​∣λ1​,⋯,λk​∈Z}.

The matrix B\mathbf BB is called basis of the lattice Λ(B)\Lambda(\mathbf B)Λ(B).

⚠️A lattice has more than one basis.

Unimodular matrices

幺模矩阵，什么玩意从来没听说过

📖Definition 1 (unimodular matrices). An n×nn\times nn×n matrix U\mathbf UU is called unimodular, if U∈Zn×n\mathbf U\in\Z^{n\times n}U∈Zn×n and det⁡(U)∈{±1}\det(\mathbf U)\in\{\pm1\}det(U)∈{±1}.

简单一句话，就是行列式等于正负 1 的方阵。

🤔Lemma 1. If U\mathbf UU is unimodular, then U−1\mathbf U^{-1}U−1 is also unimodular.

🤔Lemma 2. Let B1,B2∈Rn×n\mathbf B_1,\mathbf B_2\in\R^{n\times n}B1​,B2​∈Rn×n non-singular. Then Λ(B1)=Λ(B2)\Lambda(\mathbf B_1)=\Lambda(\mathbf B_2)Λ(B1​)=Λ(B2​) iff there is a unimodular matrix U\mathbf UU with B2=B1U\mathbf B_2=\mathbf B_1\mathbf UB2​=B1​U.

The fundamental parallelepiped

“基平行六面体”

The fundamental parallelepiped of the lattice Λ(B)\Lambda(\mathbf B)Λ(B) is the polytope

P(B)={∑i=1nλibi∣0≤λi<1∀i∈[n]}.\mathcal P(\mathbf B)=\left\{\sum_{i=1}^n\lambda_i\mathbf b_i\vert0\le\lambda_i\lt1\forall i\in[n]\right\}.P(B)={i=1∑n​λi​bi​∣0≤λi​<1∀i∈[n]}.

简单来讲，就是两个基向量在 0 到 1 的线性组合覆盖的所有区域。在二维空间是个平行四边形，在三维空间是个平行六面体。

For every vector x∈Rn\mathbf x\in\R^nx∈Rn, there is a unique coefficient vector λ∈Rn\lambda\in\R^nλ∈Rn so that x=∑i=1nλibi\mathbf x=\sum_{i=1}^n\lambda_i\mathbf b_ix=∑i=1n​λi​bi​. 高中数学知识

So x\mathbf xx can be written as

x=∑i=1n⌊λi⌋bi+∑i=1n(λi−⌊λi⌋)bi.\mathbf x=\sum_{i=1}^n\lfloor\lambda_i\rfloor\mathbf b_i+\sum_{i=1}^n(\lambda_i-\lfloor\lambda_i\rfloor)\mathbf b_i.x=i=1∑n​⌊λi​⌋bi​+i=1∑n​(λi​−⌊λi​⌋)bi​.

The left half ∈Λ(B)\in\Lambda(\mathbf B)∈Λ(B), and the right half ∈Λ(B)\in\Lambda(\mathbf B)∈Λ(B).

This translates of the parallelepiped placed at lattices points exactly partition the Rn\R^nRn. This is called a tiling of Rn\R^nRn.

平铺

Fundamental parallelepiped can be rewritten as

P(B)={Bx:x∈[0,1[n},\mathcal P(\mathbf B)=\{\mathbf {Bx}:\mathbf x\in[0,1[^n\},P(B)={Bx:x∈[0,1[n},

it is the image of the hyper-cube [0,1]n[0,1]^n[0,1]n under the linear map given by B\mathbf BB. By transformation formula

vol(P(B))=vol([0,1[n)⋅∣det⁡(B)∣.vol(\mathcal P(\mathbf B))=vol([0,1[^n)\cdot|\det(\mathbf B)|.vol(P(B))=vol([0,1[n)⋅∣det(B)∣.

Fundamental parallelepiped it self depend on the choice of the basis, but its volume doesn’t.

🤔Lemma 3. Let B∈Rn×n\mathbf B\in\R^{n\times n}B∈Rn×n and Λ=Λ(B)\Lambda=\Lambda(\mathbf B)Λ=Λ(B) be the generated lattice. Then the determinant of the lattice det⁡(Λ)=∣det⁡(B)∣\det(\Lambda)=|\det(\mathbf B)|det(Λ)=∣det(B)∣ is independent of the chosen basis. Moreover, det⁡(Λ)=vol(P(B))\det(\Lambda)=vol(\mathcal P(\mathbf B))det(Λ)=vol(P(B)).

Minkowski’s Theorem

闵可夫斯基格点定理

A set KKK is convex if ∀x,y∈K\forall x,y\in K∀x,y∈K and 0≤λ≤10\le\lambda\le10≤λ≤1, λx+(1−λ)y∈K\lambda x+(1-\lambda)y\in Kλx+(1−λ)y∈K.

A set KKK is centrally symmetric if x∈Kx\in Kx∈K iff −x∈K-x\in K−x∈K.

For convex symmetric set KKK, we define a Minkowski norm:

∣∣x∣∣K=min⁡{λ≥0:x∈λK},λK={λx∣x∈K}.\vert\vert x\vert\vert_K=\min\{\lambda\ge0:x\in\lambda K\},\\ \lambda K=\{\lambda x|x\in K\}.∣∣x∣∣K​=min{λ≥0:x∈λK},λK={λx∣x∈K}.

That is, ∣∣x∣∣K||x||_K∣∣x∣∣K​ gives the scaling factor that one needs until the scaled copy of KKK includes xxx.

Define y+K={x+y∣x∈K}y+K=\{x+y|x\in K\}y+K={x+y∣x∈K} as the translate of KKK by yyy.

Minkowski’s theorem says that every large enough symmetric convex set must contain a non-zero lattice point.

🎯Theorem 4 (Minkowski). Let K⊆RnK\subseteq\R^nK⊆Rn be a bounded symmetric convex set with vol(K)>2nvol(K)\gt2^nvol(K)>2n. Then K∩(Zn╲{0})≠∅K\cap(\Z^n\diagdown\{\mathbf 0\})\neq\emptyK∩(Zn╲{0})=∅.

🎯Theorem 5 (Blichfeldt). Let S⊆RnS\subseteq\R^nS⊆Rn be a measurable set with vol(S)>1vol(S)\gt1vol(S)>1. Then there are s1,s2∈S\mathbf s_1,\mathbf s_2\in Ss1​,s2​∈S with s1−s2∈Zn\mathbf s_1-\mathbf s_2\in\Z^ns1​−s2​∈Zn.

Minkowski’s Theorem for General Lattices

🎯Theorem 6. (Minkowski’s first theorem). Let Λ\LambdaΛ be a lattice and KKK be a symmetric convex set with vol(K)>2ndet⁡(Λ)vol(K)\gt2^n\det(\Lambda)vol(K)>2ndet(Λ). Then K∩(Λ╲{0})≠∅K\cap(\Lambda\diagdown\{\mathbf 0\})\neq\emptyK∩(Λ╲{0})=∅.

Shortest Vector

The shortest vector w.r.t. L2-norm:

SVP(Λ)=min⁡{∣∣x∣∣2∣x∈Λ╲{0}}SVP(\Lambda)=\min\{\vert\vert\mathbf x\vert\vert_2\vert\mathbf x\in\Lambda\diagdown\{\mathbf 0\}\}SVP(Λ)=min{∣∣x∣∣2​∣x∈Λ╲{0}}

is its length.

🤔In fact, finding the shortest vector is an NPNPNP-hard problem.

We can get some estimates on it.

🎯Theorem 7. Any lattice Λ⊆Rn\Lambda\subseteq\R^nΛ⊆Rn, one has SVP(Λ)≤n⋅det⁡(Λ)1/nSVP(\Lambda)\le\sqrt n\cdot\det(\Lambda)^{1/n}SVP(Λ)≤n​⋅det(Λ)1/n.

🎯Theorem 8 (Minkowski’s second theorem). For any full-rank lattice Λ∈Rn\Lambda\in\R^nΛ∈Rn, one has

(∏i=1nλi(Λ))1/n≤n⋅det⁡(Λ)1/n.\left(\prod_{i=1}^n\lambda_i(\Lambda)\right)^{1/n}\le\sqrt n\cdot\det(\Lambda)^{1/n}.(i=1∏n​λi​(Λ))1/n≤n​⋅det(Λ)1/n.

Dirichlet’s Theorem

🎯Theorem 9. A vector α∈[0,1]n\alpha\in[0,1]^nα∈[0,1]n of real numbers. There are numbers p1,⋯ ,pn∈Z≥0p_1,\cdots,p_n\in\Z_{\ge0}p1​,⋯,pn​∈Z≥0​ and q∈{1,⋯ ,Q}q\in\{1,\cdots,Q\}q∈{1,⋯,Q} so that

max⁡i=1,⋯ ,n∣piq−αi∣≤1Q1/nq.\max_{i=1,\cdots,n}|\frac{p_i}{q}-\alpha_i|\le\frac{1}{Q^{1/n}q}.i=1,⋯,nmax​∣qpi​​−αi​∣≤Q1/nq1​.

Gram Schmidt Orthogonalisation

To find approximate shortest vector.

A lattice Λ(B)\Lambda(\mathbf B)Λ(B), we want to find a non-zero vector in polynomial time that has length ∣∣x∣∣2≤α⋅SVP(Λ(B))||x||_2\le\alpha\cdot SVP(\Lambda(\mathbf B))∣∣x∣∣2​≤α⋅SVP(Λ(B)). Here α=α(n)≥1\alpha=\alpha(n)\ge1α=α(n)≥1, called approximation factor (as small as possible).

The Gram Schmidt orthogonalisation takes indepedent vectors b1,⋯ ,bn∈Rn\mathbf b_1,\cdots,\mathbf b_n\in\R^nb1​,⋯,bn​∈Rn as input and computes an orthogonal basis b1∗,⋯ ,bn∗\mathbf b^*_1,\cdots,\mathbf b^*_nb1∗​,⋯,bn∗​ so that span(b1,⋯ ,bk)=span(b1∗,⋯ ,bk∗)\text{span}(\mathbf b_1,\cdots,\mathbf b_k)=\text{span}(\mathbf b^*_1,\cdots,\mathbf b^*_k)span(b1​,⋯,bk​)=span(b1∗​,⋯,bk∗​) for all k=1,⋯ .nk=1,\cdots.nk=1,⋯.n.

Pseudo-code

Input: b1,⋯ ,bn∈Rn\mathbf b_1,\cdots,\mathbf b_n\in\R^nb1​,⋯,bn​∈Rn

Output: Orthogonal basis b1∗,⋯ ,bn∗\mathbf b^*_1,\cdots,\mathbf b^*_nb1∗​,⋯,bn∗​

  1. b1∗←b1\mathbf b_1^*\gets\mathbf b_1b1∗​←b1​
  2. b2∗←b2−μ1,2b1∗\mathbf b_2^*\gets\mathbf b_2-\mu_{1,2}\mathbf b_1^*b2∗​←b2​−μ1,2​b1∗​ with μ1,2←⟨b2,b1∗⟩∣∣b1∗∣∣22\mu_{1,2}\gets\frac{\lang\mathbf b_2,\mathbf b_1^*\rang}{||\mathbf b_1^*||_2^2}μ1,2​←∣∣b1∗​∣∣22​⟨b2​,b1∗​⟩​
  3. …
  4. bj∗←bj−∑i<jμi,jbi∗\mathbf b_j^*\gets\mathbf b_j-\sum_{i\lt j}\mu_{i,j}\mathbf b_i^*bj∗​←bj​−∑i<j​μi,j​bi∗​ with μi,j←⟨bj,bi∗⟩∣∣bi∗∣∣22∀j=1,⋯ ,n\mu_{i,j}\gets\frac{\lang\mathbf b_j,\mathbf b_i^*\rang}{||\mathbf b_i^*||_2^2}\forall j=1,\cdots,nμi,j​←∣∣bi∗​∣∣22​⟨bj​,bi∗​⟩​∀j=1,⋯,n

施密特正交化，属于是复习大一上学的线性代数了。

By Cavalieri’s principle （aka 祖暅原理）, the shifting does not change the volume of the fundamental parallelepiped. Hence,

det⁡(Λ(B))=vol(P(B))=∏i=1n∣∣bi∗∣∣2.\det(\Lambda(\mathbf B))=vol(\mathcal P(\mathbf B))=\prod_{i=1}^n||\mathbf b_i^*||_2.det(Λ(B))=vol(P(B))=i=1∏n​∣∣bi∗​∣∣2​.

🎯Theorem 10. Let B\mathbf BB be a basis and B∗\mathbf B^*B∗ be its Gram Schmidt orthogonalisation. Then SVP(Λ(B))≥min⁡i=1,⋯ ,n∣∣bi∗∣∣2SVP(\Lambda(\mathbf B))\ge\min_{i=1,\cdots,n}||\mathbf b_i^*||_2SVP(Λ(B))≥mini=1,⋯,n​∣∣bi∗​∣∣2​

第一个正交化向量总是相等的。基向量的顺序很重要，这为 LLL 算法埋下伏笔。

The Lenstra-Lenstra-Lovász Algorithm

🎯Theorem 11. Given a regular matrix \mathbf B\in\Q^{n\times n} one can compute a vector x∈Λ(B)╲{0}\mathbf x\in\Lambda(\mathbf B)\diagdown\{\mathbf 0\}x∈Λ(B)╲{0} of length ∣∣x∣∣2≤2n/2⋅SVP(Λ(B))\vert\vert\mathbf x\vert\vert_2\le2^{n/2}\cdot SVP(\Lambda(\mathbf B))∣∣x∣∣2​≤2n/2⋅SVP(Λ(B)) in polynomial time.

The running time is actually O(n6log⁡3(n∣∣B∣∣∞))O(n^6\log^3(n\vert\vert\mathbf B\vert\vert_\infty))O(n6log3(n∣∣B∣∣∞​)).

Consider basis b1,⋯ ,bn\mathbf b_1,\cdots,\mathbf b_nb1​,⋯,bn​:

  • Subtracting vectors from each other. In n=2n=2n=2, we can always subtract multiple of b1\mathbf b_1b1​ from it so that μ1,2≤1/2\mu_{1,2}\le 1/2μ1,2​≤1/2. In higher dimensions, we can achieve ∣μi,j∣≤1/2|\mu_{i,j}|\le1/2∣μi,j​∣≤1/2 for every i,ji,ji,j.
  • Switching the order. It might be that b1\mathbf b_1b1​ is much longer than b2\mathbf b_2b2​. In this case, we may swap the order of b1\mathbf b_1b1​ and b2\mathbf b_2b2​. For higher dimension, we swap bi\mathbf b_ibi​ and bi+1\mathbf b_{i+1}bi+1​ if ∣∣bi∣∣2≫∣∣bi+1∣∣2\vert\vert\mathbf b_i\vert\vert_2\gg\vert\vert\mathbf b_{i+1}\vert\vert_2∣∣bi​∣∣2​≫∣∣bi+1​∣∣2​.

Coefficient Reduction

A basis is coefficient-reduced if ∣μi,j∣≤1/2\vert\mu_{i,j}\vert\le1/2∣μi,j​∣≤1/2 for all i<ji\lt ji<j.

🤔Lemma 12. Given any basis B\mathbf BB one can compute a coefficient-reduced basis B~\tilde{\mathbf B}B~ in polynomial time so that Λ(B~)=Λ(B)\Lambda(\tilde{\mathbf B})=\Lambda(\mathbf B)Λ(B~)=Λ(B) and the Gram Schmidt orthogonalisations are identical.

Main Procedure

📖Definition of LLL-reduced: Let B∈Rn×n\mathbf B\in\R^{n\times n}B∈Rn×n be a lattice basis and let μi,j\mu_{i,j}μi,j​ be the coefficients from the Gram Schmidt orthogonalisation. The basis is called LLL-reduced if the following is satisfied.

  • Coefficient reduced: ∣μi,j∣≤1/2\vert\mu_{i,j}\vert\le1/2∣μi,j​∣≤1/2 for all i<ji\lt ji<j.
  • Lovász condition: ∣∣bi∗∣∣22≤2∣∣bi+1∗∣∣22\vert\vert\mathbf b_i^*\vert\vert_2^2\le2\vert\vert\mathbf b_{i+1}^*\vert\vert_2^2∣∣bi∗​∣∣22​≤2∣∣bi+1∗​∣∣22​ for i=1,⋯ ,n−1i=1,\cdots,n-1i=1,⋯,n−1.

🤔Lemma 13. Let B\mathbf BB be an LLL-reduced basis. Then ∣∣b1∣∣2≤2n/2⋅SVP(Λ(B))||\mathbf b_1||_2\le2^{n/2}\cdot SVP(\Lambda(\mathbf B))∣∣b1​∣∣2​≤2n/2⋅SVP(Λ(B)).

By Lovász condition, ∣∣b1∣∣22=∣∣b1∗∣∣22≤2∣∣b2∗∣∣22≤⋯≤2i−1∣∣bi∗∣∣22||\mathbf b_1||_2^2=||\mathbf b_1^*||_2^2\le2||\mathbf b_2^*||_2^2\le\cdots\le2^{i-1}||\mathbf b_i^*||_2^2∣∣b1​∣∣22​=∣∣b1∗​∣∣22​≤2∣∣b2∗​∣∣22​≤⋯≤2i−1∣∣bi∗​∣∣22​.

By Theorem 10, SVP(Λ(B))2≥2−n⋅∣∣b1∣∣22SVP(\Lambda(\mathbf B))^2\ge 2^{-n}\cdot||\mathbf b_1||_2^2SVP(Λ(B))2≥2−n⋅∣∣b1​∣∣22​.

Taking square roots then gives the claim.

Pseudo-code

Input: A lattice basis B∈Rn×n\mathbf B\in\R^{n\times n}B∈Rn×n

Output: An LLL reduced basis B~\tilde{\mathbf B}B~

  1. Compute a Gram Schmidt orthogonalisation b1∗,⋯ ,bn∗\mathbf b_1^*,\cdots,\mathbf b_n^*b1∗​,⋯,bn∗​ with coefficients μi,j\mu_{i,j}μi,j​ and update whenever we change the order of the basis.
  2. while B\mathbf BB is not LLL reduced, do
    • Apply coefficient reduction so that μi,j≤1/2\mu_{i,j}\le1/2μi,j​≤1/2.
    • If there is an index iii with ∣∣bi∗∣∣22>2∣∣bi+1∗∣∣22||\mathbf b_i^*||_2^2\gt2||\mathbf b_{i+1}^*||_2^2∣∣bi∗​∣∣22​>2∣∣bi+1∗​∣∣22​ then swap bi\mathbf b_ibi​ and bi+1\mathbf b_{i+1}bi+1​ in the ordering.

🤔Lemma 14. Suppose we have vectors a1,a2\mathbf a_1,\mathbf a_2a1​,a2​ with Gram Schmidt orthogonalisation a1∗,a2∗\mathbf a_1^*,\mathbf a_2^*a1∗​,a2∗​ so that ∣∣a1∗∣∣22≥2∣∣a2∗∣∣22||\mathbf a_1^*||_2^2\ge2||\mathbf a_2^*||_2^2∣∣a1∗​∣∣22​≥2∣∣a2∗​∣∣22​ and let μ≤1/2\mu\le1/2μ≤1/2. Let a2∗∗,a1∗∗\mathbf a_2^{**},\mathbf a_1^{**}a2∗∗​,a1∗∗​ be the Gram Schmidt orthogonalisation for the reverse order a2,a1\mathbf a_2,\mathbf a_1a2​,a1​. Then ∣∣a2∗∗∣∣2≤3/4⋅∣∣a1∗∣∣2||\mathbf a_2^{**}||_2\le\sqrt{3/4}\cdot||\mathbf a_1^*||_2∣∣a2∗∗​∣∣2​≤3/4​⋅∣∣a1∗​∣∣2​.
