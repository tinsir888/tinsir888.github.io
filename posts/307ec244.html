This week

  • Revenue-maximizing DSIC auctions
  • completely different goal than social welfare maximization

Review

Spectrum auction’s problem: VERY LOW REVENUE

Social welfare maximization

many applications

makes sense even in cases where revenue maximization seems to be more important (e.g. keeping customers compared to competitors)

educational value: ideal auctions and ideal mechanisms always exist

Single item auction with a single potential buyer

revenue?

what other truthful auctions exist?

posted prices, take it or leave it offers

is it possible to maximize revenue without knowledge of private valuations?

for example, a fixed price of 10 work fine for valuations higher than 10 while it will be disastrous for smaller prices.

Revenue maximization

Average case analysis

single-parameter environment

what’s the revenue of the second-price auction?

i.e. smallest valuation among the two.

Revenue-optimal auctions

participant iii selects random valuation from distribution FiF_iFi​.

probability distributions F\mathbf FF with continuous probability density function f\mathbf ff. defined in [0,+∞)[0,+\infty)[0,+∞)

Assumption

  1. the mechanism designer knows the distributions of valuations.
  2. the valuations of different potential buyers are independent

virtual valuations: ϕi(vi)=vi−1−Fi(vi)fi(vi)\phi_i(v_i)=v_i-\frac{1-F_i(v_i)}{f_i(v_i)}ϕi​(vi​)=vi​−fi​(vi​)1−Fi​(vi​)​

here viv_ivi​ stands for desired revenue, 1−Fi(vi)fi(vi)\frac{1-F_i(v_i)}{f_i(v_i)}fi​(vi​)1−Fi​(vi​)​ stands for discount.

Revenue and virtual valuation

Lemma: for every single-parameter environment with nnn participant having independent valuations drawn from distribution F\mathbf FF. every DSIC mechanism (x,p)(\mathbf x,\mathbf p)(x,p), every participant iii and every vector v−i\mathbf v_{-i}v−i​ of valuations

Evi∼Fi[pi(v)]=Evi∼Fi[φi(vi)⋅xi(v)]\mathbb E_{v_i\sim F_i}[p_i(\mathbf v)]=\mathbb E_{v_i\sim F_i}[\varphi_i(v_i)\cdot x_i(\mathbf v)]Evi​∼Fi​​[pi​(v)]=Evi​∼Fi​​[φi​(vi​)⋅xi​(v)]

theorem: expected revenue=expected virtual valuations

Ev∼F[∑i=1npi(v)]=Ev∼F[∑i=1nφi(vi)⋅xi(v)]\mathbb E_{\mathbf v\sim\mathbf F}[\sum_{i=1}^np_i(\mathbf v)]=\mathbb E_{\mathbf v\sim\mathbf F}[\sum_{i=1}^n\varphi_i(v_i)\cdot x_i(\mathbf v)]Ev∼F​[i=1∑n​pi​(v)]=Ev∼F​[i=1∑n​φi​(vi​)⋅xi​(v)]

proof using the lemma above and linearity of expectation.

Proof of the lemma

Revenue-optimal auction design

new goal: select an allocation rule that maximizes the virtual social welfare

Ev∼F[∑i=1nφi(vi)⋅xi(v)]\mathbb E_{\mathbf v\sim\mathbf F}[\sum_{i=1}^n\varphi_i(v_i)\cdot x_i(\mathbf v)]Ev∼F​[i=1∑n​φi​(vi​)⋅xi​(v)]

we restrict ourselves to regular distributions that have the property that the virtual valuation function φi(vi)=vi−1−Fi(vi)fi(vi)\varphi_i(v_i)=v_i-\frac{1-F_i(v_i)}{f_i(v_i)}φi​(vi​)=vi​−fi​(vi​)1−Fi​(vi​)​ is non-decreasing

then, the allocation rule that maximizes the virtual social welfare is monotone.

process: nnn potential buyer with regular distributions

  1. compute the virtual valuation ϕi(vi)\phi_i(v_i)ϕi​(vi​) of the truthfully reported valuation viv_ivi​.
  2. select the feasible allocation that maximizes the virtual social welfare ∑i=1nφi(vi)xi(v)\sum_{i=1}^n\varphi_i(v_i)x_i(\mathbf v)∑i=1n​φi​(vi​)xi​(v)
  3. charge payments according to Myerson’s lemma
