Budget constraints

limit the amount of money that an agent can pay

in practice: in a sponsored search auction, every bidder is asked for her bid-per-click and her daily budget.

small but drastic change in modeling the behavior of participant iii with budget BiB_iBi​ and valuation viv_ivi​.

A single-item auction with budget constraints

  • given the item to a random participant

  • payment = 0

  • Can we do better with DSIC auctions?

    scenario: nnn bidder for a single items. Budgets of 1 for each bidder. Is there a (nearly) social welfare maximizing DSIC mechanism?

    use a priority order of the agents if those agents of value greater than 1, give the item to agent to highest priority.

Uniform-price auction

  1. let ppp be the price that equalizes the supply with the demand, i.e., A−(p)≥m≥A+(p)A^-(p)\ge m\ge A^+(p)A−(p)≥m≥A+(p)
  2. award Di(p)D_i(p)Di​(p) items to each participant iii at price ppp.
  3. decide arbitrarily the number of items given to participants with vi=pv_i=pvi​=p so that all items are sold

some fact:

  • uniform-price auction is not DSIC:

    e.g. 2 copies, 2 agent: B1=+∞,v1=6,B2=v2=5B_1=+\infty,v_1=6,B_2=v_2=5B1​=+∞,v1​=6,B2​=v2​=5.

      so the uniform-price auction gives 2 items to participant 1 at price 5, utility = 2.

      if price < 3, the aggregate demand is A(p)≥3A(p)\ge3A(p)≥3

      when the price becomes 3, D1(3)=1D_1(3)=1D1​(3)=1 (其实这里没有定义，因为有剩余的物品，所以就给了), D2(3)=1D_2(3)=1D2​(3)=1.

      so the uniform-price auction gives 1 item to participant 1 at price 3, utility = 3.

in addition, the uniform-price auction

  • respects participants’ budgets
  • monotone allocation rule
  • wrong payments

Clinching auction

together with the current price ppp, the auction keeps track of the current demands sss (initially mmm) and the residual budget B^i\hat B_iB^i​ for every participant iii

residual demand:

D^i(p)={min⁡{⌊B^ip⌋,s}, p<vi0, p>viD^i+(p)=lim⁡q↓pD^i(q)\hat D_i(p)=\begin{cases} \min\{\lfloor\frac{\hat B_i}{p}\rfloor,s\},\ p\lt v_i\\ 0,\ p\gt v_i \end{cases} \\ \hat D_i^+(p)=\lim_{q\downarrow p}\hat D_i(q)D^i​(p)={min{⌊pB^i​​⌋,s}, p<vi​0, p>vi​​D^i+​(p)=q↓plim​D^i​(q)

the auction iteratively increases the current price and participant iii “clinches” some items at price ppp whenever they are uncontested, i.e., when the aggregate residual demand of the remaining participants is strictly lower than the current supply sss.

An example

2 items, 2 participants: B1=+∞,v1=6,B2=v2=5B_1=+\infty,v_1=6,B_2=v_2=5B1​=+∞,v1​=6,B2​=v2​=5

does the participant have any incentive to misreport? NO. we’ll proof later.

The clinching auction in addition

  • always terminates
  • allocates exactly mmm items
  • respects budgets
  • DSIC when budgets are public

why?

The allocation rule is monotone and the payments are the ones defined using Myerson’s Lemma

DSIC proof:

  • assuming public budgets, the only control participant iii has is in the moment is when she stops participating
  • every item clinched with price p<vip\lt v_ip<vi​ (p>vip\gt v_ip>vi​), contributes positively (negatively) to the utility
  • thus, truth-telling guarantees non-negative utility
  • with a false bid bi<vib_i\lt v_ibi​<vi​, the auction behaves the same up to the price p=bip=b_ip=bi​ and, then, participant iii can’t claim items she would clinch in the interval [bi,vi][b_i,v_i][bi​,vi​] and which would increase the utility.
  • with a false bid bi>vib_i\gt v_ibi​>vi​, the auction behaves the same up to price p=vip=v_ip=vi​ and any additional items participant iii may get at price >vi\gt v_i>vi​ will decrease her utility.

Mechanism design without money

House allocation

  • nnn participants, each with her own house
  • each participant has a ranking of all houses.
  • how can we redistribute the houses s.t. the participants are as happy as possible?
  • Top trading cycle (TTC) algorithm

Top trading cycle algorithm

NNN: set of participants

while N≠∅N\neq\emptyN=∅

  • construct the directed graph GGG with the participants as nodes and edge (i,j)(i,j)(i,j) if the most preferred house for participant iii is that belonging to jjj.
  • compute all directed cycles in GGG
  • for every edge (i,j)(i,j)(i,j) of ever cycle CCC, give the house of jjj to iii
  • remove the nodes of CCC from set NNN.

Some facts

  • algorithm TTC is DSIC
  • first, every participant who gets a house at round kkk
    • prefers it compared to any other house, besides the ones given in earlier rounds
    • got it from someone who also got a house in round kkk
  • proof idea: no false reporting can give to participant iii a house that was given in a previous round
  • why? in any previous round, there is no edge from someone who got a house to participant iii.
  • interesting: the algorithm that sometimes doesn’t redistribute any house is DSIC.

block coalition: a set of participants s.t. there is a way to redistribute their houses so that all prefer the new allocation

core allocation: an allocation that has no blocking coalition

The allocation computed by TTC is the unique core allocation

Proof:

step 1: an allocation of houses to agents can be in the core only if it is the outcome of the TTC algorithm

any allocation in the core should look like the outcome of the TTC algorithm regularizing the house allocated to agents of group N1N_1N1​. otherwise, N1N_1N1​ would be a blocking coalition.

step 2: it is indeed a core allocation

consider any set of agents SSS, different from N1,N2,⋯N_1,N_2,\cdotsN1​,N2​,⋯

这样会导致 SSS 中有的智能体在分配新房子的时候分到没有之前那么喜欢的房子。
