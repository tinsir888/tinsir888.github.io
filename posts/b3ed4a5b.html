Robot Motion Planning

Given a set of obstacles P1,⋯ ,PkP_1,\cdots,P_kP1​,⋯,Pk​, and a polygonal robot RRR with starting configuration sss and target configuration ttt. Find a path for the robot from sss to ttt if possible.

机器人路径规划问题，首先应该先解决：存不存在这样一条路径的问题。

Reference point

define a reference point ppp of the robot and define position of the vertices of RRR w.r.t. ppp.

Suppose our robot can move by translating

R(x,y)R(x,y)R(x,y): position of the robot when the reference point is at (x,y)(x,y)(x,y).

Suppose our robot can move by translating:

R(x,y,α)R(x,y,\alpha)R(x,y,α): position of the robot when the reference point is at (x,y)(x,y)(x,y) and the robot has orientation α\alphaα.

We represent the robot by a point in the parameter space (configuration space) R2×[0,360)\mathbb R^2\times[0,360)R2×[0,360)

先只考虑机器人只能平移，不能旋转。

partition the configuration space in free space and forbidden space

ppp in forbidden space ⇔R(p)\Lrarr R(p)⇔R(p) intersects an obstacle.

RRR can move from sss to t⇔t\Lrarrt⇔ there is a path from sss to ttt in the free space.

对于机器人上的一个参考点，在图上都有一个 free space 图。如果 free space 联通起始点，那么就存在一个路径使得机器人可以从起点移动到终点。

if RRR is a point then configuration space = work space.

Finding a path in the Configuration Space

如果存在路径，那么就找出这样一条路径

given a configuration space P1,⋯ ,PkP_1,\cdots,P_kP1​,⋯,Pk​ of complexity nnn find a path from sss to ttt if possible.

idea: construct a trapezoidal decomposition of the free space.

思路：将 free space 用竖线进行梯形分解。

construct a road map G=(V,E)G=(V,E)G=(V,E)

VVV: set of all centers of all trapezoids Δ\DeltaΔ and all midpoints of all vertical segments sss.

梯形的中位线中点+所有梯形顶边和底边线段的中点

(Δ,s)∈E⇔s(\Delta,s)\in E\Lrarr s(Δ,s)∈E⇔s is a vertical segment bounding Δ\DeltaΔ.

add sss and ttt to GGG and connect them to the trapezoids containing them.

Run a BFS in the road map to find a path from sss to ttt.

Theorem

we can compute a path from sss to ttt in O(nlog⁡n)\mathcal O(n\log n)O(nlogn) expected time.

如何搞定 free space 的计算问题呢？使用闵可夫斯基和解决！

Minkowski Sums

Let PPP and RRR be two sets in R2\mathbb R^2R2

P\oplus R=\{p+r|p\in R\and r\in R\} is the Minkowski sum of PPP and RRR.

where p+r={px+rx,py+ry}p+r=\{p_x+r_x,p_y+r_y\}p+r={px​+rx​,py​+ry​}

observation: let Q=P⊕RQ=P\oplus RQ=P⊕R, an extreme point on QQQ in direction ddd is the sum of extreme points in direction ddd on PPP and RRR.

Theorem

  1. Let PPP and RRR be convex polygons with nnn and mmm edges, then Q=P⊕RQ=P\oplus RQ=P⊕R is a convex polygon and has at most m+nm+nm+n edges.
  2. Let PPP and RRR be convex polygons with nnn and mmm edges, then Q=P⊕RQ=P\oplus RQ=P⊕R can e computed in O(m+n)\mathcal O(m+n)O(m+n) time.

Question: What if PPP and RRR are not convex?

Pseudo-discs

a pair PPP and RRR are pseudo-discs

the boundaries of PPP and RRR intersect in at most two points.

i.e.,

∂P∩int(R) is connected and∂R∩int(P) is connected.\partial P\cap int(R)\text{ is connected and}\\ \partial R\cap int(P)\text{ is connected.}∂P∩int(R) is connected and∂R∩int(P) is connected.

observation: A pair of polygonal pseudo-discs PPP and RRR defines at most two boundary crossing

Theorem

Let S\mathcal SS be a collection of convex polygonal pseudo-discs with a total of nnn edges. the total complexity of their union is at most 2n2n2n.

  • pseudo-disk vertices
  • intersection points

Charge every pseudo-disk vertex to itself.

For an intersection point q=e∩fq=e\cap fq=e∩f, by observation: either

  • eee has no other boundary crossing with \part R.
  • fff has no other boundary crossing with \part P, or both

Let eee be the edge without other boundary crossings, and let vvv be the endpoint of eee in RRR.

Charge qqq to vvv.

Claim: Every vertex vvv (of a pseudo-disk PPP) is charged at most twice.

  • Case 1: vvv not in any other pseudo-disk ∴ vvv is a green vertex and is charged only by itself.

  • Case 2: v,v∈Rv,v\in Rv,v∈R lies in the interior of the union.

    Traverse e=vw‾e=\overline{vw}e=vw. There is only one point qqq on the boundary of the union on eee that charges to vvv.

    Same for the other edge vu‾\overline{vu}vu

  • Case 3: v,v\in\part R lies on the boundary of the union. As in case 2 vvv is charged only by its incident edges eee and fff and by vvv itself

    vvv can not be charged by both its incident edges at the same time.

Let S\mathcal SS be a collection of pseudo-discs with a total of nnn edges. The total complexity of their union is at most O(n)O(n)O(n).

Observation: Let PPP and RRR be convex polygons with disjoint interiors, let d1d_1d1​ and d2d_2d2​ be directions where PPP is more extreme than RRR.

Then either

  • PPP is more extreme in all directions in [d1,d2][d_1,d_2][d1​,d2​], or
  • PPP is more extreme in all directions in [d2,d1][d_2,d_1][d2​,d1​].

Let PPP and QQQ be two convex polygons with disjoint interiors, and let RRR be another convex polygon. Then P⊕RP\oplus RP⊕R and Q⊕RQ\oplus RQ⊕R are pseudo-discs.

To show: \part P\oplus R\cap int(Q\oplus R) is connected.

Proof by contradiction. Assume \part P\oplus R\cap int(Q\oplus R) is not connected.

By observation, an extreme point on P⊕RP\oplus RP⊕R corresponds to a extreme point on PPP. Same for QQQ.

⇒P\Rarr P⇒P more extreme on dpd_pdp​ and dqd_qdq​ than QQQ

⇒Q\Rarr Q⇒Q more extreme on dsd_sds​ and drd_rdr​ than PPP

What if PPP is not convex?

observation: for any set P,Q,RP,Q,RP,Q,R

R⊕(P∪Q)=(R⊕P)∪(R⊕Q)R\oplus(P\cup Q)=(R\oplus P)\cup(R\oplus Q)R⊕(P∪Q)=(R⊕P)∪(R⊕Q)

Triangulate PPP

⇒\Rarr⇒ this produces a collection of disjoint convex polygons, so the collection of Minkowski sums is a collection of pseudo-disks.

Let PPP be a polygon with nnn vertices, and let RRR be a convex polygon with mmm vertices. Then complexity of P⊕RP\oplus RP⊕R is Θ(mn)\Theta(mn)Θ(mn).

What if PPP and RRR are both not convex?

Triangulate both PPP and RRR. This gives a collection of O(mn)O(mn)O(mn) triangles.

Let PPP be a polygon with nnn vertices, and let RRR be a convex polygon with mmm vertices, The complexity of P⊕RP\oplus RP⊕R is Θ(n2m2)\Theta(n^2m^2)Θ(n2m2).

Let PPP be a polygon with nnn vertices, and let RRR be a convex polygon with m=O(1)m=O(1)m=O(1) vertices, we can compute P⊕RP\oplus RP⊕R in O(n2log⁡n)\mathcal O(n^2\log n)O(n2logn) time.

Shortest Paths

Given a set of obstacles P1,⋯ ,PhP_1,\cdots,P_hP1​,⋯,Ph​, a starting point sss and target point ttt. Find a shortest path from sss to ttt,

Lemma A shortest pat between sss and ttt among a st of disjoint obstacles P1,⋯ ,PhP_1,\cdots,P_hP1​,⋯,Ph​ is a polygonal path whose inner vertices are vertices of P1,⋯ ,PhP_1,\cdots,P_hP1​,⋯,Ph​.

Question: how do we compute a shortest path between sss and ttt?

idea: Construct a graph G=(V,E,w)G=(V,E,w)G=(V,E,w) with edge length s.t. for (u,v)∈E(u,v)\in E(u,v)∈E the edge corresponds to the distance between uuu and vvv.

Theorem: We can compute GGG in O(n2log⁡n)O(n^2\log n)O(n2logn) time.

Visibility Graph

Connecting two vertices if they can see each other.

Sub-goal: compute all visible vertices from any point ppp in O(nlog⁡n)O(n\log n)O(nlogn) time.

i.e. visibility graph in O(n2log⁡n)O(n^2\log n)O(n2logn) time.

Idea: Radical sweep ray!

Exactly identical to normal sweep line, if we rotate the ball until ppp is a point at infinity.

我的理解是把这个看成以该点为极点的极坐标，可以映射到一个以该点为原点的直角坐标。就可以转化为最普通的扫描线了。

Find vertices visible from ∞\infty∞ X-coordinate.

Compute visible vertices from sss to ttt, add to visibility graph, then use Dijkstra for O(n2log⁡n)O(n^2\log n)O(n2logn) time in total.

(can be improved to O(nlog⁡n)O(n\log n)O(nlogn))
